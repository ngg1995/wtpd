Welcome, Patient!

This study assesses the communication of risk from medical decision support tools.

In each case you will be asked whether you want to take statins to reduce your risk of a heart attack.

Statins are a type of medication that can help lower your cholesterol levels. Cholesterol is a substance that can build up in your blood vessels, making it harder for blood to flow through. Over time, this can lead to a heart attack. By taking statins, you can reduce the amount of cholesterol in your blood, which can lower your risk of having a heart attack. Statins are commonly prescribed by doctors for people at risk of heart disease, and they have been shown to be effective in preventing serious heart problems.

It is recommended that people with a 10% chance of having a heart attack or stroke in the next 10 years are advised to take statins.

Welcome, Doctor!

Statins are widely used to help patients reduce their cholesterol levels and lower the risk of cardiovascular events, such as heart attacks. As you know, high cholesterol can lead to atherosclerosis, narrowing arteries and increasing the risk of heart disease. By prescribing statins, you can help patients reduce these risks, especially those with a history of heart disease or elevated cholesterol levels.

This study evaluates how risk information is communicated to patients using medical decision support tools.

In each case, you will be asked to provide recommendations on whether statins should be considered for reducing a patient's risk of heart attack.

There will be three possible outcomes to assess for each case.
